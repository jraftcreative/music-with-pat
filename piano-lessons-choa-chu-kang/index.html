Conveniently Close

Getting Here from Choa Chu Kang

Tengah sits right next to Choa Chu Kang, making the journey to Patricia's studio quick and straightforward. Whether you drive, take the bus, or use the MRT, most families from CCK reach the studio in around 10 minutes.

If you live near South View Primary, De La Salle School, or Unity Primary, you're already within a short distance. Families from the newer Tengah developments will find the studio particularly accessible — it's within the same neighbourhood.

House visits are also available throughout Choa Chu Kang for families who prefer lessons at home. This is especially popular with parents of younger children who find it easier to learn in familiar surroundings.

Transport Options

  • By car: Around 10 minutes from Choa Chu Kang via Choa Chu Kang Way or Brickland Road
  • By MRT: Choa Chu Kang station is on the North-South Line — connect towards Jurong East for access to the Jurong Region Line
  • By bus: Several bus services connect CCK to Tengah, including routes along Choa Chu Kang Avenue
  • House visits: Available throughout Choa Chu Kang and surrounding estates

Lesson Structure

What Lessons Look Like

Each lesson is a one-to-one session lasting 45 to 60 minutes, depending on your child's age and attention span. Younger students typically start with shorter sessions that gradually lengthen as their focus develops.

A typical session covers a mix of practical playing, sight-reading, aural skills, and music theory — all woven together in a way that keeps children engaged rather than overwhelmed. Patricia balances structured learning with creative activities so that every lesson feels purposeful and enjoyable.

For families in Choa Chu Kang, lessons are held at Patricia's home studio in Tengah or at your home through the house visit option. Either way, the focus is on creating a calm, supportive space where your child can progress at their own pace.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get to the studio from Choa Chu Kang? +
The studio in Tengah is roughly a 10-minute drive from most parts of Choa Chu Kang. By public transport, you can take the North-South Line from Choa Chu Kang MRT towards Jurong East, then connect to the Jurong Region Line. Several bus services also run between CCK and Tengah. Many families find the short trip well worth it for consistent, quality lessons.
Does Patricia offer house visits in Choa Chu Kang? +
Yes, house visits are available for families in Choa Chu Kang. This is a popular option for parents with younger children or busy after-school schedules. Lesson fees for house visits differ slightly from studio rates — feel free to get in touch for details.
What age should my child start piano lessons? +
Most children are ready from age four. At this stage, they can follow simple instructions, sit for a short lesson, and begin developing finger coordination. The initial session is always a consultation where Patricia assesses your child's readiness — there's no obligation to commit.
Are ABRSM exams compulsory? +
Not at all. ABRSM preparation is available for students who benefit from structured goals and milestones, but it's never forced. Some children progress beautifully without exams, focusing instead on building repertoire and enjoying their music. Patricia will advise on what suits your child best.
