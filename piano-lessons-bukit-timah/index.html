Location

Getting Here from Bukit Timah

Patricia's studio is located in Tengah, Singapore's newest town in the western region. For families living around Bukit Timah Road, Upper Bukit Timah, or the Sixth Avenue area, the drive is straightforward and typically takes around 15 minutes outside of peak hours.

The most direct route is via the Bukit Timah Expressway (BKE), connecting towards the Kranji Expressway (KJE) and exiting at Tengah. If you prefer to avoid the expressway, Bukit Batok Road and Jurong Town Hall Road also provide convenient surface-level routes.

Several Bukit Timah families already make the trip weekly. Many find that the dedicated journey gives their child a helpful mental shift — leaving school mode behind and arriving ready to focus on music.

The Experience

What Lessons Look Like

Every lesson is one-to-one and tailored to your child's current level, personality, and learning pace. Younger children begin with rhythm games, listening activities, and simple melodies before gradually moving into note reading and technique work. Older or more experienced students focus on repertoire building, ABRSM exam skills, and developing their own musicality.

Lessons typically run for 30 or 45 minutes depending on age and level. Patricia structures each session to cover multiple areas — a mix of pieces, technical exercises, and ear training — so progress stays balanced rather than lopsided.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is your studio from Bukit Timah? +
Patricia's studio in Tengah is roughly a 15-minute drive from most parts of Bukit Timah via the Bukit Timah Expressway (BKE). Families heading from the Beauty World or King Albert Park area can also take the Downtown Line towards Tengah. Many Bukit Timah parents find the short commute worthwhile for the quality of teaching.
Does Patricia offer house visits to Bukit Timah? +
Patricia currently teaches from her dedicated home studio in Tengah, which is set up specifically for young learners with the right instrument, acoustics, and learning materials. This consistent environment helps children settle into each lesson more quickly. She does not offer house visits at this time.
What ages do you teach, and is my child too young to start? +
Patricia teaches children from age 4 onwards. At the first consultation, she'll observe your child's attention span, motor coordination, and interest in music to determine whether they're ready for structured lessons or would benefit from waiting a little longer. There's never any pressure to commit.
How do you handle ABRSM exam preparation? +
ABRSM preparation is woven into the broader curriculum rather than treated as a separate track. Patricia builds exam skills — scales, sight-reading, aural tests, and performance pieces — into regular lessons so children feel well-prepared without the stress of last-minute cramming. She only enters students for exams when they're genuinely ready.
